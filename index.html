Executive Summary: OSA International Financial Analysis (2004)

Key Findings

This comprehensive analysis of OSA International's financial data from January through November 2004 reveals several important trends and patterns:

  1. Overall Financial Stability: Throughout 2004, OSA maintained a relatively stable weekly budget of approximately $90,000, which slightly decreased to $89,000 in the latter half of the year. The annual budget projection remained consistent at $4.6-4.7M.
  2. Financial Health Fluctuations: The organization experienced significant variations in its financial health, as measured by the bills-to-setasides ratio:
    • The ratio worsened from January (0.97) to June (1.47), indicating financial strain
    • April and August showed the strongest financial positions with ratios of 0.63 and 0.67 respectively
    • By October-November, the ratio had stabilized at 0.93, showing improvement from mid-year challenges
  3. Bureau Budget Allocations: The three main bureaus consistently received the bulk of funding:
    • Legal Bureau: Consistently received the largest allocation (40.8% on average)
    • PR Bureau: Maintained a stable allocation (27.9% on average)
    • Investigation Bureau: Slight decrease in allocation (31.3% on average)
  4. Major Recipients: The top five recipients of funds were:
    • Bisceglie & Walsh: $321,300 (25.4% of top five spending)
    • Rabinowitz Boudin: $315,981 (25.0%)
    • Zuckert Scoutt: $307,235 (24.3%)
    • Latham & Watkins: $231,169 (18.3%)
    • Grieboski: $87,600 (6.9%)
  5. Professional Retainers: Exceptionally stable throughout the year, with only minor fluctuations:
    • Total monthly retainer budget: $29,300 ($351,600 annually)
    • PR professionals received 63.1% of retainer payments
    • Legal professionals received 36.9% of retainer payments

Conclusions

The financial data indicates that OSA International navigated through varying financial conditions in 2004, with mid-year challenges (particularly June) followed by recovery in the second half of the year. The emphasis on legal activities remained constant, while significant shifts occurred in creditor relationships. The organization maintained stable professional retainer relationships throughout the period, suggesting continuity in key external partnerships despite financial fluctuations.

The improvement in financial health metrics by the end of the analyzed period suggests that corrective measures taken during the mid-year financial strain were effective, positioning the organization on a more sustainable financial trajectory entering 2005.
